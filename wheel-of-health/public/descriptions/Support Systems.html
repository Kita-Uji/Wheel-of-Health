Support systems represent the environment you operate within. From a physical point of view, it’s everything from your office desk to how you stock your pantry. On a larger scale, its community, accountability structures, and the standards you’re exposed to daily.

Why it matters:
Humans adapt to their surroundings. Willpower alone is unreliable, but environments compound behaviour. The right systems reduce friction and raise your baseline without constant effort.

Low score means:
You rely entirely on self-discipline. Progress feels isolating and fragile. You feel everyone around you is against your greater goals, and your home environment is a minefield waiting to sabotage you. 

High score means:
You are supported, challenged, and reinforced by people and systems that expect more from you. Standards are clear, and you have cultivated a space designed to push you to success. 

You rise or fall to the standards of the room you spend time in. Behaviour is shaped less by intention and more by what is normalised around you.
