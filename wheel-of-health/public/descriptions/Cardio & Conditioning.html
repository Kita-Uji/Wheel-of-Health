Endurance represents your cardiovascular capacity and your ability to sustain effort without rapid fatigue. It governs how long you can physically and mentally operate before performance degrades.

Why it matters:
Endurance improves recovery between efforts, supports fat loss, strengthens heart health, and expands overall work capacity. A higher endurance base allows you to do more with less perceived effort, and recover faster when demands accumulate.

Low score means:
You fatigue quickly, avoid conditioning, or feel limited during prolonged activity. Physical effort feels disproportionately taxing, leading to hesitation or avoidance.

High score means:
You recover quickly between efforts, maintain output over time, and don’t fear exertion. Physical demands feel manageable rather than draining.

Endurance determines whether effort feels sustainable or suffocating.
